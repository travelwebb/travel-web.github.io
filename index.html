Switzerland

The beautiful Zurich

I Avant-garde: A word that aptly defines Zurich for its arts and literary works; it’s the birthplace to Dada, it’s the origin of the innovative Freitag bags, and a place which brought into existence the designers' much-loved typeface, Helvetica. Zurich is home to many leading companies who introduce cutting-edge innovation to the world. Some of them are the world’s widely used search engine – Google, the creator of the world’s happiest theme parks – Disney, and even the acclaimed Swiss Federal Institue of Technology (ETH).

The calm city Basel

Basel with more than 40 museums in the city, Basel prides itself as the highest concentration of museums in the country. Globally acclaimed museums such as the Basel Art Museum, dedicated to the iron sculptor Jean Tinguely; the Foundation Beyeler; and the Museum of Culture, attract a vast number of visitors, just like many galleries and playhouses. Basel is also home to the symphony orchestra and the chamber orchestra along with the musical theatre featuring global productions. A variety of classical and modern productions are also performed on the stages of the Basel Theatre and the Playhouse.

The mountains of Zermatt

Zermatt couches a special place in the hearts of mountaineers. Among the many famous pistes, Haute Route is one of the most challenging international tracks that take several days to finish, leading from Mont Blanc to Zermatt. More than 400 kilometres of hiking tracks lead through and out of the Matter Valley. It also includes the mule traders' pistes, which date back to 1300s.
